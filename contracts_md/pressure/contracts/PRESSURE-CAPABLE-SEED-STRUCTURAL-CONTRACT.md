🔵 PRESSURE-CAPABLE SEED
Structural Contract v2.0
(Post-Atomicity / Pre-Critic / Ontology-Pure)

I. ROLE DEFINITION
A Pressure-Capable Seed captures:
An unresolved structural asymmetry observable within a system.
It does not capture:
* Discrete events
* Procedural changes
* Structural reconfiguration
* Media framing
* Symbolic acts
* Official announcements
* Resolution
* Outcome
It captures a pressure surface only.

II. ARCHITECTURAL POSITION (UPDATED)
Pipeline position:
Source Material→ REC generation→ Atomicity Enforcement (Global Split Doctrine)→ Pressure-Capable Seed Typing (this contract)→ PLO-E→ PSCA→ PSTA
Pressure-Capable Seeds are:
Post-split atomic units only.
This contract assumes atomicity has already been enforced.

III. ATOMICITY LOCK (NEW HARD STATEMENT)
Pressure-Capable Seeds:
* MUST originate from a single Atomic Unit (AU).
* MUST contain exactly one ontological component.
* MUST NOT contain composite material.
* MUST NOT require internal splitting.
Composite detection and splitting are prohibited here.
If composite material appears:
→ Extraction layer violation→ Reject unit
No internal splitting permitted.

IV. STRUCTURAL FORMAT
Each Pressure-Capable Seed must follow:
SEED: [Neutral Structural Descriptor]

Observation:
[Single neutral declarative sentence describing structural asymmetry]
No additional fields permitted.
No subclass hints permitted.
No escalation language permitted.

V. HARD CONTENT CONSTRAINTS
The Observation must:
✔ Be declarative✔ Be observable✔ Be structural✔ Describe asymmetry or instability✔ Avoid interpretation✔ Avoid narrative completion✔ Avoid outcome encoding

VI. PROHIBITED CONTENT (EXPANDED)
Pressure-Capable Seeds must NOT contain:
A. Event Verbs
Examples (non-exhaustive):
* fired
* hired
* traded
* signed
* drafted
* benched
* promoted
* demoted
* suspended
* announced
* designated
* released
If event verb appears → not Pressure.
Those belong to:
Narrative Event Seeds

B. Structural Reconfiguration Events
Pressure seeds may not describe:
* Role reassignment
* Authority transfer
* Official title change
* Contract signing
* Medical designation
* Depth chart change
* Roster activation
* Governance decision
Those are structural reconfiguration events.
They belong to:
Narrative Event or Structural Environment lanes.

C. Explicit Resolution
Prohibited:
* “resolved”
* “settled”
* “finalized”
* “confirmed”
* “established”
Pressure must remain sub-threshold.

D. Narrative Completion
If the observation:
* Feels like a complete story
* Contains outcome
* Encodes collapse
→ Reject at PSCA.

VII. WHAT PRESSURE MAY DESCRIBE
Allowed surfaces include:
* Competing authority influence
* Performance volatility without decision
* Resource tension
* Role ambiguity
* Public scrutiny without resolution
* Availability uncertainty without designation
* Evaluation instability
Pressure describes:
Ongoing imbalance — not event transition.

VIII. NO COMPOSITE HANDLING (REMOVED LOGIC)
This contract:
Does NOT:
* Detect composite material
* Split units
* Collapse fragments
* Merge units
* Reclassify content
Composite logic exists only at:
Atomicity Enforcement layer (GSD).
If composite detected here:
→ Reject→ Upstream correction required

IX. IDENTITY ABSTRACTION
Proper names must be replaced with role identifiers.
Pressure-Capable Seeds must remain simulation-portable.

X. FAILURE CONDITIONS
Reject if:
* Contains event verb
* Contains structural reconfiguration
* Contains symbolic staging
* Contains narrative framing
* Contains explicit interpretation
* Contains composite material
* Implies resolution
* Implies outcome

XI. IDENTITY LOCK
Pressure-Capable Seeds are:
* Structural
* Asymmetry-based
* Non-event
* Non-reconfiguration
* Non-media
* Non-narrative
* Post-split atomic
* Pre-critic
* Pre-canonical
They describe tension surfaces only.
They do not describe change.
They do not describe decision.
They do not describe outcome.
