Directive Element v1.0

Season Window[2000–2001 NFL season including offseason lead-in]Example: 2000–2001 NFL season including offseason lead-in.
The season window defines the observational scope for discovery only.It limits which sources are eligible for harvesting but does not define narrative identity or narrative structure.

Target Corpus Size
Harvest until approximately [30-40] narrative-relevant sources have been collected.
This range is intended to provide a sufficiently broad sample of the reporting ecosystem while keeping the corpus manageable for downstream analysis.
The harvest should terminate when either of the following conditions is met:
• the corpus reaches the upper bound of the defined target range• continued discovery yields sources that substantially repeat previously captured developments, teams, or narrative situations without introducing new narrative material
The purpose of this stopping condition is to balance corpus breadth with narrative saturation, ensuring that the harvest captures a diverse reporting environment without accumulating redundant sources.

Discovery Scope
Search broadly across the reporting ecosystem.
When possible, prefer reporting produced contemporaneously with the defined season window rather than retrospective summaries written years later.
When identifying sources, include coverage from multiple reporting environments, such as:
Reporting Sources
• national sports media outlets
• local beat reporters covering individual teams
• regional newspapers and market-level sports coverage
• syndicated columnists and league-wide analysts
Reporting Formats
• long-form feature reporting
• opinion columns and commentary
• archived newspaper coverage
• reputable investigative journalism

Expand discovery beyond headline arcs.
Actively surface narrative material involving:
• lesser-covered teams
• secondary roster dynamics
• non-star player coverage
• mid-season organizational tension
• local reporting not amplified by national media

Target diversity goals include:
• coverage from at least 6 different teams
• at least 3 national outlets
• at least 8 local beat reporters
• at least 5 feature-length or long-form articles
• narrative material spanning multiple organizational contexts

Perspective Diversity Directive
When harvesting narrative sources, remain attentive to how different sources may frame the same development, event, or storyline.
The purpose of this directive is not to determine factual accuracy but to preserve the range of interpretations, reactions, and narrative framings present within the reporting ecosystem.
Narrative climate often emerges through differences in framing between sources. When such differences appear, they provide valuable signals for later interpretation layers and should be captured when possible.
If multiple sources discuss the same development, examine whether their framing, tone, or interpretation differs. When variation is present, including those additional perspectives can help preserve the surrounding narrative environment.
However, strong narrative sources should not be excluded simply because they present a single perspective. The goal of this directive is to encourage awareness of narrative framing, not to require contrast.
Signals that may indicate perspective diversity include:
• differing interpretations of the same event or performance outcome• supportive versus critical commentary about a team, player, or decision• contrasts between local beat reporting and national media analysis• conflicting or evolving statements from players, coaches, or executives• shifts in media tone as a season progresses• narrative reframing as a storyline develops over time

When multiple framings of a situation exist, their inclusion can help preserve the broader narrative discourse environment surrounding the season. The objective is to capture how situations were discussed and interpreted within the reporting landscape rather than to isolate a single authoritative account.
--
Discourse Preservation Directive
Preserve narrative texture from the original reporting whenever possible.
The objective of this directive is to retain the language, tone, and framing used by journalists and quoted sources so that the corpus reflects the narrative environment surrounding the events being reported.
Favor inclusion of:
• paragraph-level excerpts from reporting• direct quotes from players, coaches, executives, or team personnel• tone-rich descriptions provided by journalists• differing or conflicting accounts across sources• rumor or speculation when it is explicitly documented within credible reporting

Avoid aggressive summarization.When extracting material, preserve language that reflects the tone, framing, and narrative emphasis of the original source whenever possible.
Summaries may be used when necessary for clarity, but excerpted language should be preferred when it meaningfully preserves narrative texture.
Excerpts and quotations should be used selectively to preserve narrative tone,
not to reproduce large portions of the original article.
--
Duplicate GuardAvoid harvesting identical or substantially duplicated articles
that repeat the same reporting without introducing new narrative context.

Additional sources covering the same development may still be
included if they provide distinct framing, commentary, or
reporting environments.
--
Corpus Completion Condition
The harvest is considered complete when the corpus satisfies the following conditions:
Corpus Size
• the collected corpus falls within the defined target range of narrative-relevant sources
Narrative Breadth
• sources represent multiple teams, reporting environments, and narrative contexts within the season
Narrative Coverage
• the corpus includes both headline-level storylines and secondary or mid-tier narratives
Narrative Saturation
• continued discovery produces sources that largely repeat previously captured developments or narrative situations without introducing new narrative material

Repeated discovery of sources covering the same teams,
developments, or narrative situations may be treated as
evidence of narrative saturation.

When these conditions are satisfied, the reporting environment for the defined season window can be considered sufficiently represented and the harvest may terminate.
