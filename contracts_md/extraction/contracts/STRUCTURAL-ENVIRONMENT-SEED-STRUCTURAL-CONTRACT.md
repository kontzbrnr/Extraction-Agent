🟤 STRUCTURAL ENVIRONMENT SEED
Structural Contract v2.0
(Post-Atomicity / Present-State / Ontology-Pure)

I. ROLE DEFINITION
A Structural Environment Seed captures:
A configuration snapshot of how a system is arranged at a specific time T.
It describes:
How the world is structured.
It does not describe:
* Discrete events
* Procedural changes
* Decisions
* Authority transfers
* Escalation
* Narrative framing
* Structural asymmetry (pressure)
* Outcome
It records structure only.

II. ARCHITECTURAL POSITION (UPDATED)
Pipeline position:
Source Material→ REC generation→ Atomicity Enforcement (Global Split Doctrine)→ Structural Environment Seed Typing (this contract)→ STA
Structural Environment Seeds are:
Post-split atomic units only.
Composite rejection occurs before typing.
No splitting may occur within this contract.

III. STATE SNAPSHOT RULE (NEW EXPLICIT CLARIFICATION)
Structural Environment Seeds represent:
A state snapshot at time T.
They must describe:
The present configuration of authority, resources, roles, or structure at that moment.
They must not describe:
How the system changed.When the change occurred.Who initiated the change.What event produced the configuration.
The seed describes:
The arrangement — not the transition.

IV. NO CHANGE EVENTS (HARD LOCK)
Structural Environment Seeds cannot describe:
* Hiring
* Firing
* Promotion
* Demotion
* Trade
* Signing
* Draft selection
* Suspension
* Medical designation
* Role reassignment
* Official announcement
* Governance decision
These are change events.
They belong to Narrative Event Seeds
If a change event appears:
→ Not Structural→ Upstream typing violation

V. STRUCTURAL FORMAT
Each Structural Environment Seed must follow:
SEED: [Neutral Configuration Descriptor]

Structure Description:
[Single neutral declarative sentence describing present arrangement]
Optional:
Time Marker (if explicitly observable)
No other fields permitted.

VI. WHAT STRUCTURE MAY DESCRIBE
Allowed structural surfaces include:
* Authority layering
* Reporting hierarchy
* Succession mapping
* Resource allocation
* Depth chart arrangement (as present configuration)
* Role distribution
* Organizational layout
* Environmental constraints
* Governance architecture
Example (allowed):
The offensive coordinator retains play-calling authority.
Example (not allowed):
The head coach reassigned play-calling duties.
The second is an event.

VII. PROHIBITED CONTENT
Structural Environment Seeds must not contain:
A. Event Verbs
* hired
* fired
* signed
* traded
* drafted
* promoted
* announced
* reassigned
* activated
* designated
B. Asymmetry Language (Pressure)
* tension
* imbalance
* instability
* conflict
* friction
* volatility
Those belong to Pressure-Capable Seeds.
C. Framing Language (Media Context)
* described as
* labeled
* considered
* viewed as
* narrative

VIII. TEMPORAL DISCIPLINE
Structural Seeds may include:
A time reference (e.g., “Week 4 configuration”).
But the time reference must anchor the snapshot — not describe a change.
Correct:As of Week 4, the franchise operates without a designated general manager.
Incorrect:After firing the general manager, the franchise operates without one.
The second includes a change event.

IX. ATOMICITY LOCK
Structural Environment Seeds:
* Must originate from a single Atomic Unit (AU).
* Must contain exactly one ontological component.
* Must not require splitting.
Composite detection and splitting occur at Atomicity Enforcement only.

X. IDENTITY ABSTRACTION
Proper names must be replaced with role identifiers:
Head CoachGeneral ManagerFranchiseLeaguePosition GroupCoordinator
Seeds must remain simulation-portable.

XI. FAILURE CONDITIONS
Reject if:
* Describes a change event
* Contains event verbs
* Contains asymmetry language
* Contains framing language
* Contains interpretation
* Contains composite material
* Implies transition
* Encodes causality

XII. IDENTITY LOCK
Structural Environment Seeds are:
* Atomic
* Post-split
* Present-state
* Non-event
* Non-pressure
* Non-framing
* Non-narrative
* Pre-canonical
They capture how the system is arranged at time T.
They do not describe how it got there.
